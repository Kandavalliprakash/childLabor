About Child Labour

Child labour refers to the exploitation of children through any form of work that deprives children of their childhood, interferes with their ability to attend regular school, and is mentally, physically, socially or morally harmful.

Issues

Child labour exists worldwide, with millions of children engaged in hazardous work in agriculture, mining, manufacturing, and domestic service.

Child Labour Statistics

Global Impact

According to the International Labour Organization (ILO), there are approximately 152 million children worldwide engaged in child labour.

Sectoral Distribution

71% of child labourers work in the agricultural sector, often exposed to hazardous conditions.

Gender Disparity

Girls are disproportionately affected, with 58 million girls engaged in child labour compared to 94 million boys.

Regional Variations

Sub-Saharan Africa is the region with the highest incidence of child labour, with over 1 in 5 children engaged in labour.
